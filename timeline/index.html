Conceptualization Phase

Preliminary, our goal is to learn more about existing ground check procedures, and the issue gaps they fail to address.

Early testing phase

With the launch of alpha and beta testing, we hope to identify key weak points in usability and redefine our mission accordingly.

outreach phase

After gathering critical feedback, we need to reach out to startup incubators and conservation forums to build up a userbase and springboard our growth.

Preliminary Field Research

Identified a problem: existing ground check methods lag behind technologically, accrue high time and operational costs, are inaccessible to forestry services with limited human resources, and lack the level of ubiquity necessitated by broad-scale recovery initiatives.

October 2018

Inception of Pinesight

Modeling industry solution begins. Pinesight will adopt the tenants of the modern technology industry – decenralization, privacy, and transparency. Pinesight will achieve a level of usability and interconnectivity unlike any other environmental conservation app on the market.

November 2018

Prototype App Development

Prototype app completed for demonstration purposes featuring rudimentary classifier with limited support for augmented image characteristics. Proof of concept used to identify weak points in the original idea, which in turn provide anchors to pivot.

December 2018

Business Plan

Gathering of industry related stats, including competitor research and market research surveying. Four factors that current ground check methods fail to address effectively: accessibility, classification accuracy, low time and resource constraints, and tracking on a broadscale. Most projects seek to gate audience reach, spam ads and force membership fees, and harvest data from their users.
